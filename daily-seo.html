Leanne's Daily SEO Checklist

Simple things you can do every day that actually move the needle

Why this matters

You're already putting images on Google - that's great. But Google (and AI search like ChatGPT) needs more than photos. They need words, reviews, and fresh activity to know you're still the best limo service in Brisbane. These daily tasks take 15-20 minutes and they compound over time. Consistency beats perfection.

Every Day (pick 1-2, rotate through them)

Post a photo to Google Business Profile with a caption

You're already uploading photos - amazing. The one change: add a caption every time. Google reads captions to understand what the photo is. A photo with no caption is invisible to search.

Instead of: Just uploading the photo

Do this: Upload + write: "Our white Bentley Continental ready for Sarah's wedding at Stamford Plaza Brisbane today. milimo provides luxury wedding car hire across Brisbane and the Gold Coast."
2 minutes

Reply to every Google review (even old ones)

Go to your Google Business Profile and reply to any review you haven't responded to yet. Google sees replies as a sign you're active and engaged. Be personal - use their name, mention the event.

Example reply: "Thank you so much Jessica! It was our pleasure to be part of your wedding day. Adrian loved driving the Bentley to Hillstone St Lucia - what a beautiful venue. We hope married life is treating you well! - The milimo family"
3 minutes per review

Post one photo or short video to Instagram

Doesn't need to be fancy. A 10-second video of a car looking beautiful. A photo from today's job. A Reel of Adrian polishing the Bentley. Use 3-5 hashtags and a location tag every time.

Always use these hashtags:
#brisbanewedding #brisbanelimo #weddingcarbrisbane #luxurycarbrisbane #milimo

Always tag location: Brisbane, Queensland / Gold Coast / the venue name
5 minutes
After Every Wedding or Event

Ask the client for a Google review

This is the single most important thing you can do. Send a text message the next day with your Google review link. Keep it simple and warm.

Text template: "Hi [name]! It was such a pleasure being part of your special day yesterday. If you have 30 seconds, a Google review would mean the world to our little family business. Here's the link: [your Google review link]. Thank you! - Leanne & Adrian, milimo x"
1 minute to send

Take 3-5 photos at every job

The car at the venue. The car with the couple (with permission). A detail shot of the interior or ribbons. These become your Google and Instagram posts for the week. One event = 3-5 days of content.

2 minutes at the event
Once a Week (pick a quiet day)

Write a Google Business Profile update post

Go to your Google Business Profile and click "Add Update". Write a short post about what's happening. Google loves fresh activity.

Post ideas (rotate through these):
- "Wedding season is here! Book your Bentley or Genesis G80 for 2026/2027. Call 1300 884 536"
- "Another beautiful Gold Coast wedding this weekend. We love what we do"
- "Did you know milimo also does airport transfers? Door-to-door luxury from Brisbane Airport"
- "School formal season is coming! Stretch limo packages available for groups"
5 minutes

Check your Google Business Profile Q&A section

People sometimes ask questions on your Google listing. If there are any unanswered ones, reply to them. You can also add your own Q&As - this helps AI search find your answers.

Add these Q&As yourself if they're not there:
Q: "How much does wedding car hire cost in Brisbane?"
A: "milimo offers luxury wedding car hire packages tailored to your day. Pricing depends on vehicle choice, duration, and travel distance. Call us on 1300 884 536 for a personalised quote."
5 minutes
Golden Rules

Always mention Brisbane, Gold Coast, or Sunshine Coast

Every caption, every post, every reply - mention the location. Google connects your business to these areas when you do. "Wedding car hire" is generic. "Wedding car hire Brisbane" is what people actually search.

Name the car in every photo

Don't just upload a photo. Say "Our Bentley Continental Flying Spur" or "The Genesis G80 in white". People search for specific cars. Google reads your words.

Name the venue when you can

If you had a wedding at Hillstone St Lucia or Victoria Park, mention it. People search "wedding car hire [venue name]" and you already have 30+ venue pages on your website. Connect the dots.

Consistency beats volume

One photo a day with a good caption beats 10 photos dumped with no words. Google rewards regular activity over bursts of silence.

Don't upload photos with no words

A photo with no caption, no alt text, no description is invisible to Google and AI. Always write something - even one sentence is better than nothing.

Don't ignore Google reviews

An unanswered review tells Google you're not active. Reply to every single one - even a simple thank you. The more you reply, the higher Google ranks you.

What You're Already Doing Right

You're uploading photos to Google - keep going!

Most businesses don't even do this. You're already ahead. The only tweak is adding captions with keywords (Brisbane, wedding, Bentley, etc). That turns invisible photos into search magnets.

Your reviews are incredible

5.0 on TripAdvisor, 4.9 on Easy Weddings. That's genuinely rare. The goal now is to get those reviews happening on Google too - that's where most people search first.
